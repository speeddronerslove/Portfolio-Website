# MOGANA MURALI — PORTFOLIO SCENE MAP

Built on the Master Design Bible V1.0. Eight scenes, one continuous experience. No resume. No grid. Every reveal earned.

Narrative arc:
Who is this person? → This is interesting → Wait, he did that? → He does that too? → This person has range.

---

## SCENE 1 — ARRIVAL

**Purpose**
First impression. Establish tone before a single fact is shared.

**Content to display**
- "MOGANA MURALI" — full-bleed, oversized typography
- One short line. Not a job title. Not "passionate developer." A single statement of presence (e.g. a one-line philosophy fragment, decided later — never a label)
- Faint scroll cue, nothing animated or playful

**Emotional goal**
Curiosity. Stillness. "Who is this?"

**Transition into next scene**
Name dissolves to near-black. The orange gradient ignites faintly at the edge of frame like a light source switching on elsewhere in the room — pulling the eye forward.

**Visual direction**
- Pure #000000 / #050505 field
- Single light source, no full-screen color wash
- Type is the only hero element — no photo, no icon, no card
- Slow parallax drift on background texture only, nothing else moves

---

## SCENE 2 — IDENTITY

**Purpose**
Introduce the human, not the résumé. No titles yet — Full Stack, Game Dev, AI Builder are still withheld.

**Content to display**
- A short editorial paragraph (2–3 lines max) about who he is as a builder/creator — mindset, not occupation
- No bullet list of skills, no role labels
- Optional: a single still image or abstract visual representing him, not a headshot grid

**Emotional goal**
"This is interesting." Quiet intrigue, not excitement yet.

**Transition into next scene**
Text fades on a beat. Gradient expands slightly wider across the frame, as if the light source is moving closer — signaling that the story is about to go back in time.

**Visual direction**
- Generous negative space, text never spans full width
- Secondary text color (#9A9A9A) used for supporting line, primary (#FFFFFF) for the statement
- No card, no border — just space and type

---

## SCENE 3 — JOURNEY

**Purpose**
Show the path that built him — CS student, the discovery of craft — as a story arc, not a timeline widget.

**Content to display**
- BE CSE academic foundation, framed as origin, not credential
- The moment(s) where curiosity turned into discipline — entry into building things, entry into 3D/game art
- Told as a short narrative sequence (2–3 reveals), not a vertical progress line with dots

**Emotional goal**
Relatability and grounding — the audience starts rooting for him before any achievement appears.

**Transition into next scene**
The narrative line trails off mid-sentence, visually — pulling toward an image that expands to fill the frame, where the first hard proof point is waiting.

**Visual direction**
- Sequential text reveals on scroll, each replacing the last (not stacked)
- Image expansion used once here — a single meaningful visual growing to full width as the journey "arrives" somewhere
- No timeline rail, no numbered steps, no icons

---

## SCENE 4 — MILESTONES

**Purpose**
Deliver the first hard proof. This is the "wait, he did that?" beat.

**Content to display**
- Gold Medalist — Tamil Nadu Skills 2025, 3D Digital Game Art
- Treated as a singular cinematic moment, not one card among many — full attention on this one fact
- Minimal supporting line of context (what it represents, not a paragraph of explanation)

**Emotional goal**
Genuine surprise and respect — the turn from "interesting person" to "this person is exceptional."

**Transition into next scene**
The achievement visual holds, then slowly desaturates and recomposes into a new full-width frame — implying "and that's only the beginning of what he's built."

**Visual direction**
- One scene, one fact — nothing else competes for attention
- Large typographic moment: "GOLD MEDALIST" treated with the same weight as the hero name
- Full-width imagery if available (the work itself, not a certificate scan)
- No percentage meters, no counters, no badge grid

---

## SCENE 5 — WORK

**Purpose**
Reveal technical range — Full Stack Development and AI Automation — as proof of building, not a tech-stack inventory.

**Content to display**
- Full Stack Development, introduced through what was built and what it enabled — not a list of languages/frameworks
- AI Automation Builder identity, introduced the same way — outcomes and intent, not tool names
- One or two selected pieces of work shown large, not a portfolio grid

**Emotional goal**
"He does that too?" — range starts to register, but calmly, not as a flex.

**Transition into next scene**
Work visuals fade and pull leftward/inward, as though clearing the stage — gradient brightens slightly, signaling the most personal, most visual scene is next.

**Visual direction**
- Full-width or large-format imagery per project, generous spacing between
- Text kept short — a line of context per piece, never a paragraph
- Absolutely no tech-stack grid, no skill bars, no service-card layout

---

## SCENE 6 — CREATIVE SIDE

**Purpose**
The peak reveal. Game Development and his signature creation, HapticVerse, shown as the emotional high point of the whole experience.

**Content to display**
- Game Developer identity — 3D world-building, game art craft, shown through visuals rather than description
- HapticVerse introduced as his most ambitious creation — what it is, what it feels like to experience, the idea behind it (content to be finalized with Mogana directly — this scene deserves real footage/renders, not placeholder text)
- This is the scene with the most visual weight in the entire site

**Emotional goal**
"This person has range." Awe. The scene the visitor will remember and screenshot.

**Transition into next scene**
Visuals slowly contract and the gradient dims toward the edges, easing the intensity down — signaling a shift from spectacle to reflection.

**Visual direction**
- Largest imagery/video moments on the entire page
- Most generous use of the orange gradient as atmosphere — but still light, never a full wash
- If a card is ever justified anywhere on the site, this is the one scene it could appear — rgba(255,255,255,0.03) fill, rgba(255,255,255,0.08) border, 32px radius, large padding, used sparingly

---

## SCENE 7 — FUTURE

**Purpose**
Shift from "what he's done" to "where he's going" — close the achievement arc with intent rather than a list.

**Content to display**
- A single short vision statement — what he's building toward next
- No roadmap, no list of goals, no "currently learning" badges

**Emotional goal**
Quiet anticipation. The visitor leans forward instead of leaning back.

**Transition into next scene**
The statement fades, frame empties, gradient recedes almost entirely back to black — the room is dimming as the conversation winds down.

**Visual direction**
- Maximum white space of any scene so far
- One centered or asymmetric line, large type, then nothing
- No imagery — this scene is built entirely from restraint

---

## SCENE 8 — CONTACT

**Purpose**
Close the experience with an invitation, not a form dump.

**Content to display**
- Name reprised, smaller this time — a quiet echo of the opening
- Contact method(s) and relevant links, presented minimally
- No social icon row, no floating icons

**Emotional goal**
Calm, warm closure — an open door, not a hard stop.

**Transition into next scene**
None — final scene. The page simply settles into stillness.

**Visual direction**
- Centered, minimal, generous margins
- Same typographic system as Scene 1, lower intensity
- Gradient nearly fully receded — back to near-pure black, light source extinguished

---

## NOTE ON HAPTICVERSE

Scene 6 is the structural and emotional peak of the site. Its content placeholders above are intentionally light — that scene will only land if it's built from real visuals/footage of HapticVerse rather than descriptive text. Worth treating as its own mini design pass once assets are ready.
